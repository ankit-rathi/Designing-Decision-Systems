# BOOK CONTEXT (Persistent Concept Layer)

This book explains how organizations transform observations into decisions through data systems.

The fundamental process is:

Reality → Data → Intelligence → Decision → Action → Outcome → Learning

Organizations that successfully implement this loop become **learning systems that improve decisions over time**.

The book is organized into six conceptual parts:

### Part I — Foundations

Why decision systems exist.

### Part II — From Reality to Data

How organizations observe and capture real-world events.

### Part III — From Data to Intelligence

How data becomes analysis, insights, and predictions.

### Part IV — From Intelligence to Decisions

How predictions become operational decisions.

### Part V — Learning Systems

How decisions generate feedback and continuous improvement.

### Part VI — Building the Data Organization

How organizations build infrastructure and culture for decision intelligence.

Each chapter advances this conceptual model.

Do not repeat explanations unnecessarily.

# CORE DEFINITIONS (Use Consistently)

Use these definitions consistently across chapters.

**Reality**
The real-world environment in which organizations operate.

**Data**
Recorded observations of real-world events.

**Intelligence**
Insights, patterns, or predictions derived from data.

**Decision**
A structured choice among possible actions.

**Action**
The operational execution of a decision.

**Outcome**
The real-world result of an action.

**Learning**
The improvement of future decisions based on observed outcomes.

1. What is the **central problem** this chapter explains?
