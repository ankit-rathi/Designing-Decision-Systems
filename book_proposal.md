# **Marketing description**

Most organizations today invest heavily in data and AI, yet struggle to translate that investment into measurable business value. Dashboards are built, models are deployed, and pipelines scale—but decisions often remain unchanged.

*From Data to Decisions* reframes this problem. Instead of focusing on building data systems in isolation, it introduces a unified framework for designing systems that improve decision-making. At its core is the Decision Intelligence Loop: a system that connects real-world observations to data, transforms data into intelligence, and embeds that intelligence into decisions, actions, and measurable outcomes.

This book provides a practical and conceptual guide for data professionals, product leaders, and business decision-makers to design systems that not only process data—but continuously learn and improve decisions over time.

---

# **About the topic**

Organizations today generate unprecedented amounts of data and are rapidly adopting AI technologies. However, a large proportion of data and AI initiatives fail to deliver measurable business value.

The root cause is structural: most systems are designed around data pipelines and models, not decisions and outcomes.

This book introduces Decision Intelligence Systems as a unifying architecture that connects data, intelligence, and decision-making into a closed-loop system. It explains how organizations can move beyond data collection and model building to create systems that learn from outcomes and continuously improve decisions.

This shift—from data systems to decision systems—is critical for organizations seeking to unlock real value from AI.

# **Audience**

**Level:** Intermediate

The primary audience for this book is **data engineers and experienced data professionals** who design and build data systems. These readers are familiar with data pipelines, storage systems, and basic machine learning concepts, but often lack a structured framework for connecting their work to business decisions and outcomes.

Secondary audiences include **product managers and business leaders** who are responsible for decision-making but seek a deeper understanding of how data and AI systems can support and improve those decisions.

Readers are expected to have:

* Basic SQL and data system knowledge
* Familiarity with analytics and/or machine learning concepts
* Exposure to business metrics and product thinking

They are likely to have engaged with modern data stack tools and may have read books on data engineering or machine learning systems.
